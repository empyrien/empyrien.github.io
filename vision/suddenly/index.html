The Hour

“There shall suddenly appear…”

The prophecy, then two centuries in conversation. The interpretation is labeled speculation; the quotes are real.

“The world is in travail, and its agitation waxeth day by day… And when the appointed hour is come, there shall suddenly appear that which shall cause the limbs of mankind to quake. Then, and only then, will the Divine Standard be unfurled, and the Nightingale of Paradise warble its melody.”
Bahá'u'lláh, Gleanings LXI
Interpretation is personal speculation

What appears suddenly, causes the limbs of mankind to quake, and — only then — turns humanity toward a Standard it had ignored? I read this, speculatively, as a fit for transformative artificial intelligence: a capability arriving with unprecedented suddenness, unsettling every institution at once, and forcing the species to ask, in unison and under deadline, what do we actually value?

Two centuries, one conversation

On the left, texts from 1863–1920. On the right, the people building AI — Demis Hassabis and Dario Amodei, interviewed together by The Economist at the Paris AI Action Summit, February 2025.

Bahá'u'lláh · Gleanings XCIX
“The vitality of men's belief in God is dying out in every land; nothing short of His wholesome medicine can ever restore it.”
Demis Hassabis · 2025
The two great risks: bad actors — and AI “not having the right values or goals. Both of those things are critical to get right, and I think the whole world needs to focus on that.”
Bahá'u'lláh · Gleanings IV
“Soon will the present-day order be rolled up, and a new one spread out in its stead.”
Demis Hassabis · 2025
Calls for a “CERN for AGI” and an IAEA-like agency — ideally “it would be something like the UN, but given the geopolitical complexities, that doesn't seem very possible.”
'Abdu'l-Bahá · Tablet to The Hague, 1920
“In this matter, knowledge alone is far from sufficient: a power of implementation is needed… the power of conscience is therefore needed.”
Dario Amodei · 2025
“These kinds of decisions are too big for any one person… we somehow have to find a way to build a more robust governing structure.”
'Abdu'l-Bahá · Tablet to The Hague, 1919
“Material civilization is like a lamp-glass. Divine civilization is the lamp itself and the glass without the light is dark.”
The Economist's moderator · 2025
Asked whether — as with the UN after 1945 — it takes a disaster before minds change.

The world's most capable engineers are publicly asking for a shared moral framework, global institutions, and a reason for the species to come together — and saying they don't know where those come from. The Bahá'í Writings prophesy that humanity, after its material order falters, goes searching for precisely this and finds it already written. That is the connection this page exists to put in front of you, for your own investigation.
